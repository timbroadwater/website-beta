Who We Are

Tim Broadwater / “UX Unicorn”

A native of North Central West Virginia, Tim has over 20+ years of experience in UX. He has worked with various nonprofits – Fort New Salem Foundation, Marion County Historical Society, the Burgh Bears, Hill Hounds, etc. – developing an expertise in content strategy, user interface design, front-end development, UX research, and business strategy.

Some of Tim’s skills include Bear Power, Coffee Consumption, Data Crunching, Social Media Addiction, and Tattoos.

Denise Hardman / “Crazy UX Ninja Cat Lady”

Denise was born and raised in the flatlands of Kent, Ohio. She got a rocky start to her career, working as a geologist for over four years before discovering her true calling in UX. With over six years experience, specializing in UX research methods, Denise has supported a variety of nonprofits including EcoWatch, The Portage County Animal Protective League, Humane Animal Rescue, The Pittsburgh Civic Light Opera, the National Aviary, and Hope Haven Farm.

Some of Denise’s skills include a mild obsession with cats, crafting fiber stuff, drinking tea, an addiction to online shopping, gaming, and watching bad movies!

Zack Pontious / “UX Lumberjack Bidness Man”

Zack grew up in Elk County, Pennsylvania. Art was always a passion for him which he studied in undergrad and graduate school. He traveled all over the United States in his 1999 Toyota Camry (RIP) and lived in Salt Lake City and New Orleans along the way. He always considered Western Pennsylvania home and now resides in beautiful Pittsburgh. UX allows Zack to bring many of his passions and skillsets together. He likes to focus design, creative problem solving, and strategic thinking.

Some of Zack’s skills include serial entrepreneur and idea making, art making, Intermediate guitar skills, and beard growth.

Kevin Voller / “The UX Philosopher”

Kevin comes from the rural outskirts of Pittsburgh where he grew up tinkering with inventions of all sorts. He continued to pursue diverse interests through college, graduating magna cum laude with degrees in Philosophy and Technology. For the past 5 years he’s worked across academia, nonprofits, and companies big and small helping to establish and grow user-centered thinking and UX teams.

Some of Kevin’s skills include UX evangelism and teaching, juggling lots of hobbies (19 and counting), running fast, and getting philosophical about things you didn’t think could be philosophical.
